Internal review deck · Monday working session

Legacy is the container.

Three mood-board directions for Tergar Retreats, each treating legacy as the central organizing claim: a life of deep practice, made real, together, on land, inside lineage, and accountable to time.

2076 is watching.

The campaign should not ask whether the center looks persuasive in 2026. It should ask whether the practitioners of 2076 will feel that the vessel was built with enough courage.

1year to build the physical shell
3years for the first long retreat cohort
100+years as a living institutional asset

Strategic frame

Five pillars, one circular argument.

None of the directions isolates a single pillar. Each direction is a different braid of place, human practice, transmission, and evidence, held by the meta-gesture of circles, vessels, nets, and containers.

Land / Place

Iberian stone, light, season, and the building emerging from the ground. Place is proof, not scenery.

People

The cohort, lay and monastic practitioners, founder warmth, and bodies held in real discipline.

Practice

Silent meals, walking meditation, daily rhythm, and schedule as ritual rather than logistics.

Lineage

Mingyur Rinpoche, authorized teachers, sacred objects, and the thousand-year transmission without cult posture.

Research / Science

Neuroscience credibility, Davidson and Lutz collaborations, and data accumulating across decades.

Legacy as circle

What the directions refuse

Not temple-orange. Not wellness beige. Not ancient-left, science-right.

The task is to lead the mandala visually without making Tergar Retreats look imported, decorative, or guru-centered. The deck therefore keeps Tibetan symbolism restrained, makes Portugal legible, designs multilingualism into the system, and treats time as circular whenever possible.

Observatory

Ancient practice becomes a century of evidence without becoming a lab brand.

The center is where practice becomes knowledge over time.

This direction foregrounds Land, Research, and Lineage because it makes Tergar Samadhi uniquely defensible to tech, science, and systems-minded donors. It refuses the split-screen cliché of “ancient wisdom meets modern science.” Instead, the same ground holds retreat, authorized transmission, and longitudinal observation across decades.

Honest seams

What makes each direction hard.

A · The Hundred-Year Vessel

  • Can become too architectural before the facility exists.
  • Needs construction photography and material process, not just aerials.
  • If over-minimal, it risks looking like luxury hospitality instead of practice infrastructure.

B · The Cohort Is the Monument

  • Current library may not yet contain enough deep-practice imagery.
  • Group photography can slide into nonprofit sentimentality fast.
  • Founder warmth must stay specific and ordinary, never devotional branding.

C · The Living Observatory

  • “Ancient wisdom meets modern science” is already a cliché.
  • Needs real citations and study details before client-facing use.
  • Must avoid lab stock, brain icons, dashboards, and tech-conference aesthetics.
